Problem-space discovery: Causality gardening tool

A structured-yet-organic method for exploring unclear problems through open-ended causal mapping.

This facilitation tool helps you run remote Causality Garden sessions, combining the systemic breadth of Ishikawa diagrams with the depth of the 5 Whys technique.

What you can do

  • Create and manage workshop sessions
  • Capture participant responses through guided prompts
  • Automatically detect and split multiple ideas
  • Generate follow-up questions to deepen exploration
  • Discover emergent themes and clusters
  • Export insights and reflections

The 5 phases

  1. Seeding (5-10 mins) — Identify the initial feeling or observation
  2. Growing roots (20-30 mins) — Explore first-level causes freely
  3. Deepening branches (20-30 mins) — Follow promising lines of causality
  4. Clustering (30-40 mins) — Move from chaos to meaning
  5. Reflection (10-20 mins) — Surface insights and next questions

Total time: 90 minutes (individuals) to 2 hours (groups)

Typical use cases

  • Career or life reflection
  • Service discovery before problem definition
  • Group reflection on shared frustration
  • Sense-making after research or crisis
